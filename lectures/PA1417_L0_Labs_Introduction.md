# PA1417 Basic System Verification
## Labs/Assignments, Tutorials, and the Testing System

## Lab Goals
The overall goal of the labs and assignments will be to demonstrate an understanding of the lecture content and apply techniques in a real scenario.
By the end of the course you will be capable of:
1. Designing systematic test cases
2. Implementing test cases of various granularity
3. Applying relevant technology (like PyTest and Cypress)

The labs are mandatory in order to pass the course and the best preparation for the exam. The assignment descriptions will be disclosed on Canvas. Each lab will be graded with pass/fail + our feedback (The two labs contribute 2.5hp each to the final grade). The Online Q/A slots can be used to ask questions on the assignments.

## Tutorial Sessions
* Live-Coding together on campus
* An opportunity to work on assignment-like tasks together
* Work together to learn important course concepts

## Teamwork
All labs have to be worked on and submitted by groups of two.
* Find your partner during this week. You may use the Finding Lab Partners Canvas discussion.
* Communicate expectations and constraints to find a lab partner suited for you.
* Assign yourself to groups in the People section of the Canvas course.
* If you cannot find a partner by end of next week, but still want to participate in the Labs/Assignments, email lmo@bth.se and we will assign you to a group.
* You will submit all labs and receive all grades as a group.
* Students in groups must be from the same program (Campus vs Distance)

## Structure of Labs and Assignments
Each lab consists of three individual assignments:
* One assignment contains multiple exercises, which are sometimes related
* An exercise contains multiple deliverables, which are all the items your submission needs to contain to be complete
The final lab must be submitted via Canvas as individual PDF files by your group. Each lab will be graded as pass or fail according to the criteria detailed on the course page.

### Structure of an Assignment
The assignment contains three exercises. All concepts will be discussed in the previous lecture. The three exercises are related to each other and test different skills. An exercise contains a description and a list of deliverables (i.e., sub-questions). Answer all of the questions to complete your submission. In case you need to consult sources outside of the course material (lectures & tutorials), clearly reference the source and discuss the reliability of these sources.

## Test System
We will be providing a toy system to experiment on using your testing knowledge.
* We are using common technology (React, JS, Node, MongoDB, Docker) to represent a typical web application.
* The system will contain defects you are supposed to find through testing.
Repository: https://github.com/bth-dipt-teaching/bsv-edutask/
